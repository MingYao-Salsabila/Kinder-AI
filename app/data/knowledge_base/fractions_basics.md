# Fractions: Parts of a Whole

A fraction is a way to show part of a whole. Fractions are written with two numbers and a line between them, like 1/2 or 3/4.

The bottom number is called the denominator. It tells how many equal parts the whole is divided into. The top number is called the numerator. It tells how many of those parts we are talking about.

For example, if a pizza is cut into 4 equal slices and you eat 1 slice, you ate 1/4 of the pizza. The denominator (4) shows the pizza was cut into 4 equal parts, and the numerator (1) shows you ate 1 of those parts.

Fractions can describe the same amount in different ways. For example, 1/2 and 2/4 represent the same amount, even though they look different. These are called equivalent fractions.

When the numerator and denominator are the same number, like 3/3 or 5/5, the fraction equals 1, which means the whole thing. When the numerator is 0, like 0/4, the fraction equals 0, which means none of the whole.

## Key vocabulary
- Numerator: the top number in a fraction, showing how many parts are counted.
- Denominator: the bottom number in a fraction, showing how many equal parts make a whole.
- Equivalent fractions: different fractions that represent the same amount, like 1/2 and 2/4.

## Try this
Cut a piece of paper into 4 equal parts. Color in 1 part. What fraction of the paper is colored? What fraction is not colored?
