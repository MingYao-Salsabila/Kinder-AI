# The Water Cycle

Water on Earth is always moving. It travels from oceans, lakes, and rivers into the sky, falls back down as rain or snow, and then flows back to the water again. This journey is called the water cycle.

The water cycle has four main steps. The first step is evaporation. The sun heats up water in oceans, lakes, and rivers, and some of that water turns into an invisible gas called water vapor and rises into the air.

The second step is condensation. As water vapor rises higher, the air gets cooler. The cool air causes the water vapor to turn back into tiny droplets of liquid water, which gather together and form clouds.

The third step is precipitation. When the droplets in a cloud become too heavy, they fall back to the ground as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.

The fourth step is collection. Water that falls collects in oceans, lakes, and rivers, or soaks into the ground, where it can be used by plants or stored underground. Then the cycle starts again with evaporation.

## Key vocabulary
- Evaporation: liquid water turning into water vapor (a gas) because of heat.
- Condensation: water vapor turning back into liquid water droplets, forming clouds.
- Precipitation: water falling from clouds as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.
- Collection: water gathering in oceans, lakes, rivers, and underground.
