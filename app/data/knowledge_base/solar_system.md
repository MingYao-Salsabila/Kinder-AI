# Our Solar System

The solar system is made up of the Sun and everything that orbits, or travels around, it. This includes eight planets, many moons, and smaller objects like asteroids and comets.

The Sun is a star at the center of the solar system. It is much, much bigger than any planet, and its gravity is strong enough to hold all the planets in their orbits. The Sun gives off light and heat that makes life possible on Earth.

The eight planets, in order from closest to the Sun to farthest, are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars are called the inner planets because they are rocky and closer to the Sun. Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune are called the outer planets, and they are much larger and made mostly of gas.

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only planet we know of that has liquid water on its surface and living things. Earth takes about 365 days, or one year, to orbit the Sun, and it spins all the way around once each day, which gives us day and night.

## Key vocabulary
- Orbit: the path a planet or moon takes as it travels around another object, like the Sun.
- Star: a giant ball of hot, glowing gas that makes its own light, like the Sun.
- Inner planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars -- rocky planets closer to the Sun.
- Outer planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune -- large planets made mostly of gas.
