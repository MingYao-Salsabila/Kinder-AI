# Plants and Photosynthesis

Plants are living things that make their own food. They do this through a process called photosynthesis. Photosynthesis means "putting together with light."

Leaves are like tiny food factories. Inside each leaf are green parts called chloroplasts, and chloroplasts contain a green substance called chlorophyll. Chlorophyll captures sunlight and gives leaves their green color.

To make food, a plant needs three things: sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide from the air. The plant's roots soak up water from the soil, and tiny holes in the leaves let carbon dioxide in. Using energy from sunlight, the plant turns water and carbon dioxide into sugar (the plant's food) and oxygen.

The oxygen made during photosynthesis is released into the air, and that is part of the oxygen that people and animals breathe. This is one reason trees and plants are so important for life on Earth.

## Key vocabulary
- Photosynthesis: the process plants use to turn sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide into food and oxygen.
- Chlorophyll: the green substance in leaves that captures sunlight.
- Carbon dioxide: a gas in the air that plants take in.
- Oxygen: a gas that plants release, which people and animals breathe.
