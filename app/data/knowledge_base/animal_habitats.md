# Animal Habitats

A habitat is the natural home of a plant or animal. It gives the animal everything it needs to survive, like food, water, shelter, and space to move around.

Different animals live in different habitats because their bodies are suited, or adapted, to those places. A polar bear has thick fur and a layer of fat to stay warm in the cold Arctic. A camel can go a long time without water and has wide feet that help it walk on sand in the desert.

A forest habitat has many trees and is home to animals like deer, owls, and squirrels. These animals find food, build nests, and hide from danger among the trees.

An ocean habitat is full of salt water and is home to fish, dolphins, octopuses, and many other sea creatures. Ocean animals often have fins, gills, or smooth bodies that help them swim and breathe underwater.

A desert habitat is dry and can be very hot during the day and cold at night. Animals like snakes, lizards, and camels have special features that help them survive with little water.

When a habitat is damaged, for example by pollution or cutting down too many trees, the animals that live there can lose their food, shelter, or safe space. This is why protecting habitats is important.

## Key vocabulary
- Habitat: the natural home of a plant or animal, providing food, water, shelter, and space.
- Adaptation: a special feature that helps a living thing survive in its habitat.
- Pollution: harmful substances that can damage habitats and hurt living things.
